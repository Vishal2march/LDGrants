2016/2017 PROPOSAL EVALUATIONS DISCRETIONARY GRANT PROGRAM

 

 

Before evaluating proposals reviewers should read the Discretionary Grant Guidelines and Application booklet. Factors to be considered in evaluating and ranking applications are established by law and regulations, and fall into five major categories:

 

1)   institutional commitment to conservation/preservation;

 

2)   accessibility of collections to the public;

 

3)   research value of material to be preserved;

 

4)   soundness of the plan of work;

 

5)   institutional contribution to the project.

 

In addition, you are asked to make a general evaluation of the budget for the project, and to assign an overall score to the proposal.

 

The "Project Description" portion of the application is composed of six sections, including a description of the institution or agency, and sections corresponding to the five evaluation criteria listed above.  (No rating is required for section I of the Project Description, "Description of Institution or Agency.")  The specific information the applicant is asked to provide in each section is described in the Application Instructions (beginning on page 16 of the guidelines booklet).  While the criteria are of complementary importance, particular attention should be given to the research value of materials and the soundness of the plan of work when ranking the proposal. 

 

All ratings and comments are to be completed through the Division of Library Development Online Grant System.  Paper evaluations will not be accepted.  To insure the confidentiality of applicant and reviewers we ask that you not discuss your evaluations or the applications.  In order to be considered in the final review all evaluations must be completed in a timely manner.
